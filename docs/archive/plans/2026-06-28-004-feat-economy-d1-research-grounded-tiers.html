What the research changed

  • Military splits across three tiers — it was a flat “rare.” Routine transports you can actually catch (C-130) are rare; strategic/combat (B-52, B-1, fighters, AWACS) are epic; icons (B-2, U-2) legendary. Your “leans epic” instinct is right for the special military — calibrated below.
  • Vanishing narrowbodies are a real rare→legendary layer — they were all “common.” The A318 (4 left) and 737-200 (~9 left) are epic; the 727 is effectively extinct → legendary. Your A318 / 737-200 call, expanded with fleet numbers.
  • Several current pins are wrong — the 727 is pinned uncommon but it’s extinct; the 717 and MD-80s are pinned uncommon but are now rare; the MD-11 is pinned rare but it’s all-cargo and scarce → epic. See §fixes.
  • P-51 is abundant for a warbird (100+ airworthy) → rare, not epic.

The catchability caveat — read this before the tables

Half the epic/legendary list can’t actually be caught

Catches come from ADS-B + MLAT. Many of the rarest airframes broadcast no trackable signal — combat aircraft in operational config (B-2, U-2, F-22, fighters), and extinct/grounded types (An-225 destroyed, Concorde, SR-71). They will essentially never appear in catches today. So tiering them is aspirational / historical / future visual-ID, not a live chase.

Implication: the realistically catchable top of the game is the vanishing airliners (A318, DC-9, MD-11, A340 — all broadcast ADS-B ✓) plus the occasional MLAT military (C-130, B-52 near a base). That’s where the live engagement is — and it’s exactly what this research lets us tier well.
