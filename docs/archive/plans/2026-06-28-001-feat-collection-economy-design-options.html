Read this first — what the research changed

Three things the backlog got wrong, now corrected:

  • Points already surface at the AR label, Hangar card, detail view, and the reveal card back; the profile already reads server-authoritative standing. So the “make points legible / show standing” goals are largely done — the real work is the model and the moment.
  • The “ContentView still uses a string classifier” divergence is already fixed (the live AR tier is typecode-first, pinned by tests). The remaining divergence is Sets.swift nil-typecode entries.
  • The bronze→platinum medals are gone. PR #56 shipped a binary/chain trophy system. So “review the medal tiers” is a fresh fork, not a tune-up — and it’s where your new “bonus points for trophies” idea lands.

One structural fact drives the whole plan: tiers come from a single source (tools/generate-aircraft-types.py → AircraftTypes.json, bundled by iOS and ingested by the backend), and a proven re-score lever (scoring_version + npm run rescore) re-prices every existing catch on command. Re-balancing is safe and reversible.

01 · The engagement rubric

You asked me to weigh the options by what makes the economy work as a game, not just what’s accurate. Here’s the lens I’m grading every option against — six dynamics a collection economy has to serve.

① Every catch pays

A common still feels like progress — no “that was worthless” catch. The motivation killer is a zero. Diminishing returns on duplicates, never nothing.

② The chase is legible

Points track real rarity and the reveal says why. The player trusts the number — which is the north-star (“is the catch real?”) felt at the economy layer.

③ Variety > grind

First-of-type and set bonuses reward breadth, so you can’t just farm the same Cessna. Collection depth is the metric that should climb.

④ Fair anywhere

Berkeley and Bali both offer a satisfying chase. Rarity is relative to a sane baseline — not whatever happens to fly over SFO.

⑤ Consistent & anti-inflation

Adding planes or re-tiering never silently re-prices the board. Versioned, transparent, one-command re-score.

⑥ Milestones burst

On top of the steady drip, trophies and sets deliver punctuated spikes — unlock moments (and maybe a points payout) that mark real progress.

Decision 1 (model) serves ①②④⑤ · the bonus layer serves ③⑥ · Decision 2 (reveal) serves ②⑥ · Decision 3 (trophies) serves ③⑥.

02 · Decision 1 — The scoring model

How is a plane’s tier decided, how do tiers become points, and how does the whole thing scale as the catalog and the sky grow? Three approaches, graded on the rubric.

strong partial weak — graded on Accuracy · Scalability · Engagement · Build cost

A · Refined curated simplest

Keep the hand-authored tier table (clean up buckets + overrides), one re-balanced flat value per tier. No frequency input.

Accuracy Scale Engage Build

Engagement read: designer controls the “fun” tiers, but with no ground truth it can feel arbitrary (dents ② trust) and unfair across regions unless hand-managed. Scales only by someone editing the table per plane.

C · Hybrid recommended

Curated table is the floor; an offline ADS-B frequency signal nudges tiers where data is solid; editorial override always wins. Points = tier bands + a bonus layer.

Accuracy Scale Engage Build

Engagement read: designer sets the felt tiers (a beloved-but-common warbird can still be made special), data keeps them honest and fair. Self-scales through the proven re-score lever — no new realtime pipeline. Best balance of trust, control, and cost.

B · Data-derived most accurate

Tier = percentile of real ADS-B sighting frequency + editorial override. Points via log-inverse-frequency snapped to bands.

Accuracy Scale Engage Build

Engagement read: maximally fair and self-balancing — but cold: a plane people love but that’s objectively common scores nothing, and regional skew can produce odd spikes. Needs a new backend frequency pipeline with regional baselines. Biggest bet.

Why hybrid wins on engagement

Pure data (B) is the “accurate” instinct, but accuracy isn’t the same as fun — a spotting game wants a designer’s thumb on the scale so an A380 or a military jet feels like an event even if a frequency table would shrug. Pure curation (A) loses the trust that makes the chase feel earned. Hybrid keeps the felt drama and grounds it in real frequency, and crucially it reuses the re-score machinery you already have — so it ships without building a live analytics pipeline first. You can start it 95% curated and turn up the data influence later.

The tier → points shape (all models share this skeleton)

Whatever the source, points snap to tier bands — the tier sets a floor/ceiling, and a small within-band bump (flat in A, frequency-driven in B/C) places you inside it. Band-snapping is what delivers rubric ⑤: adding aircraft only ever changes a plane’s tier assignment, never silently re-prices everything else.

The bonus layer — where most of the engagement lives

Base tier points are the steady drip (rubric ①②). The bonuses are what reward the behaviors we want — breadth, completion, habit, milestones (③⑥). Proposed layers, each a separate toggle so we can tune independently:

Open sub-decision — duplicate decay

This is the biggest feel change and worth your read: today a 50th Cessna is worth the same as the first, so the leaderboard rewards grinding one spot under a flight path. Decay fixes ③ but it’s a visible nerf for high-volume spotters. Options: (a) decay to a small floor (recommended — every catch still pays), (b) decay only above a daily cap, (c) no decay, lean on first-of-type alone. Which feels right?

04 · Decision 3 — Trophies: architecture + bonus points + the unlock moment

Three coupled choices: the progression architecture, whether trophies pay out points (your new idea), and the unlock moment when one fires.

3a · Architecture — binary vs tiered climb

Keep binary + progress recommended

Matches what shipped (PR #56) + your “hard/rare is right” taste. Add a progress bar to every locked trophy, calibrate thresholds from real catch data, ship new awards as badges. Smallest risk, no migration.

Restore tiered climb richer · costlier

Each family is a ladder you climb — richer long-game. But it re-introduces the medal system PR #56 removed, needs a migration, and risks the “too many tiers” trap (Yu-Gi-Oh’s 12 rarities are a known failure). Bigger bet for unclear payoff right now.

3b · Bonus points for trophies — your new idea

Today trophy thresholds read off catch counts but trophies pay nothing — they’re pure status. Making them pay couples milestones to the economy (rubric ⑥) and gives the unlock moment real weight. The catch is where the points go — get this wrong and the leaderboard becomes about grinding trophies, not catching planes.

① Into total + board recommended

Trophy bonuses add to your one points total and the leaderboard. Simple, one number, milestone spikes are visible to everyone. Risk: a big back-catalog of trophies could lurch the board on first release — handled by the same dry-run re-score + “your score changed” note.

② Separate prestige track

Trophy points feed a second “prestige / spotter level” distinct from the catch leaderboard. Keeps the board purely about catching; adds a progression axis. More UI, two numbers to explain.

③ No points — moment only

Trophies stay status; the reward is the unlock animation, not points. Cleanest economy, but leaves your “bonus points” instinct on the table.

My lean: ① with modest, hand-set payouts scaled to difficulty (e.g. a “100 catches” trophy pays less than a “catch a legendary” trophy), so it rewards achievement without out-earning the catching itself.
