Principles

Anticipation > the number

The dopamine is in the build, not the digits. Invest the seconds before the points land.

Legible — say why

A rare catch shows why it’s rare. The economy earns trust when the player can see the reason.

Drama scales with tier

A Common is a quick beat; a Legendary is the full cinematic. Effort tracks rarity, so rare stays special.

The hero flow — anticipation, then receipt

Build-up → tier badge slams in → points count up → the “why” line + first-of-type bonus → tap to flip to the itemized receipt. Shown for an epic catch (Airbus A318, the “Baby Bus” — 4 left):

Fork 1 Lean: anticipation front, receipt on flip. This is the flow you asked for — drama up front, full transparency on tap. The refined-current card (today’s card + a why-line, no new animation) stays the low-risk fallback if the sequence proves fiddly on device.

The card — front and receipt back

The resting card after the animation. Front carries the identity + headline points; the flip side is the receipt.

Drama scales with tier

One reveal engine, five intensities. A Common is a half-second beat so a busy session doesn’t drag; a Legendary fills the screen. The effort the moment spends matches what the catch is worth.

Fork 2 Lean: scale intensity by tier. Keeps the rare moments rare and the common ones fast.

The “why it’s rare” line

For rare and above, the reveal shows a one-line fleet fact — the same research that set the tiers, now surfacing as the reason. It does two jobs: teaches the spotter something, and makes the points feel earned rather than arbitrary (principle 2).

Build note

This needs a short curated reason string per rare-and-above type (~80 types) in the same generated table that holds the tier. Small, additive, and it doubles as the source of the “catch it before it’s gone” retiring hook. Common/uncommon catches show no line — keeps the moment clean.

Fork 3 Lean: yes, for rare+. It’s the cheapest thing that makes the economy feel true.

Multi-catch — when one tap bags several

The catch zone can hold more than one plane. Today that’s MultiCatchReveal. Proposal: a compact stack you can flick through, with the combined total up top and each card openable.

Fork 4 Lean: refine the stack; full reveal for the rarest only.
