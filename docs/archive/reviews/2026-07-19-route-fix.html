The fix, in one idea

A route is only attached if the plane could actually be flying it. Every candidate route (or leg of a multi-leg filing) is now checked against the plane's observed position and track:

  • CORRIDOR the plane must be near the leg's great-circle path (within max(250 km, 15% of leg length) mid-leg; 250 km past an endpoint). A plane over Fremont is not flying Midland→Dallas — SWA1067's stale filing now yields no route instead of a wrong one.
  • TRACK among plausible legs, the plane's ground track must point at the leg's destination (within 55°, decisively beating the runner-up by 40°). Your UAL1375 — descending over Fremont, tracking ~300° at SFO — picks ONT → SFO; the same plane climbing out northeast later picks SFO → ORD.
  • Ambiguity → honest null. Never fabricate a journey no one observed (same doctrine as the round-trip work from 07-11, which this generalizes).

Where it applies

  • Live feed (/v1/aircraft enrichment): every plane, every poll — new catches get the right leg.
  • Catch-time resolve: now sends the plane's live position + track to /v1/routes.
  • Hangar backfill: sends the catch's observer position (you were within slant range of the plane), and runs per-row now — two catches sharing a callsign can be different flights on different legs.

Repairing your existing catches

On next Hangar open, CatchBackfill.clearImplausibleRoutes checks every stored route offline (bundled airports.json coords) against where you caught it. A route whose corridor you were nowhere near gets cleared and re-resolved with position. Guardrails:

  • Tolerance = the server's gate plus the catch's slant distance — a route the server approves can never be re-cleared (no flapping).
  • Endpoints missing from the bundled table → row left alone (can't judge, don't touch).
  • One-sided as-observed routes stay untouchable moment-data, as before.

What to look for in the field

  • Arrivals into SFO with through-flight filings should card the arrival leg.
  • Some previously-routed cards will go routeless — that's the corridor gate working; a missing route is now much more trustworthy than before.
  • If a plainly-visible route is missing on a plane mid-corridor, that's worth a report — the tolerance may need widening.
