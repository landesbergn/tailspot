The axis: sky presence, not prestige

Rarity here means how often you’d actually see one overhead — not how new, expensive, or impressive the plane is. An A380 is rare because few fly, not because it’s big. A brand-new 737 MAX is common because they’re everywhere. Review every call below against that one question: how often is this thing in the sky above a spotter?

Source: the curated tier logic in tools/generate-aircraft-types.py (aircraft_rarity + RARITY_OVERRIDES), which feeds AircraftTypes.json → the app and the backend.

Seven questions to settle

Each is a judgment call the curated model makes today. Confirm, or tell me to change it.

1 · Widebodies are all “uncommon” — even daily workhorses biggest call

A 787-9, 777-300ER, A350, A330, 767 are uncommon (20 pts) — the same as a hard-to-see one — while a 737 MAX is common (10). At a hub like SFO you see widebodies constantly. Calling every widebody “uncommon” makes the tier mean “is a widebody,” not “is actually scarce.”

Keep: widebody = one notch up, simple.  ·  Refine (my lean): current-service workhorse widebodies (777/787/A350/A330/767) → common; keep the scarce ones (747/A340/MD-11) at rare. Then “uncommon” genuinely means less-frequent.

✓ Decided: refine. Lean was: refine — it makes the tier honest. Costs a handful of per-type calls.

2 · Military is all “rare”

Every military type is rare (50) unless pinned up to epic/legendary. For most spotters that’s right — military is genuinely occasional. But you field-test near the Bay (and travel), and near a base a C-130 or P-8 can be routine.

Keep (my lean): military = rare; it’s scarce for the typical spotter and keeps the rule simple.  ·  Split: common transports (C-130, C-12) → uncommon, strategic stays rare+. More nuance, more upkeep.

Lean: keep rare.

3 · Helicopters are “uncommon”

All rotorcraft → uncommon (20). Over Oakland/Berkeley, police / news / medevac helis are a regular sight — arguably common. Over much of the country they’re genuinely uncommon.

Keep (my lean): uncommon — localized but lower-volume than airliners.  ·  Common: if your felt experience is “helis are everywhere here.” Your local read wins this one.

Lean: keep uncommon — but you’ve got the better data here.

4 · Business jets are “uncommon”

All bizjets → uncommon (20), with heavy/ULR ones (G650, Global) pinned up to rare. A light Citation and a Phenom sit at uncommon. Bizjets are less frequent than airliners but common near GA fields.

Keep (my lean): uncommon, heavy ones rare. Seems right.  ·  Adjust: if you think common light jets should be… common.

Lean: keep.

5 · Do you agree with the marquee pins?

The override list is judgment — worth a sanity check on the headline calls:

  • A380 = epic (not legendary). You see them at big hubs; legendary is for the truly singular.
  • 747-400 = rare; 747-8 = epic. Reasonable?
  • G650 / Global 7500 = rare. The flagship bizjets as rare — agree?
  • Anything you’d add? (e.g. a livery, a type you care about that’s mis-tiered.)

Lean: the pins look sound; flag any you’d move.

6 · Legendary is nearly empty — add the icons?

Only B-2 resolves to legendary today. Air Force One (VC-25), U-2, SR-71, and Concorde are pinned but aren’t in the active type database, so they never appear. If someone ever catches one, it wouldn’t tier correctly.

Add (my lean): inject those few designators into the type data so the grail tier actually has reachable members.  ·  Leave: accept legendary is mostly aspirational/empty.

Lean: add the handful — a legendary tier you can’t reach is no fun.

7 · General / non-commercial aviation — warbirds score like trainers your question

GA is 85% of the catalog — 2,215 of 2,612 types. Of those, 1,977 are common and 238 are uncommon — and those 238 are almost entirely helicopters (plus the King Air / PC-12 / TBM turboprops). Zero GA types are rare, epic, or legendary. So the whole fixed-wing GA world tops out at common — meaning a P-51 Mustang, Spitfire, B-17, and DC-3 are all “common” (10 pts), same as a Cessna 172.

Trainers, light singles/twins, and homebuilts (Van’s RVs, Pitts, Extra) should be common — that’s honest sky presence. But a flying warbird or a vintage classic is a genuinely rare, special sight a spotter would treasure, and right now it scores like a trainer. Same coarse-logic gap as the widebodies, on the GA side.

Keep flat: all GA common; texture comes from collecting distinct types + first-of-type. Simplest.  ·  Add a warbird/vintage layer (my lean): keep the GA long tail common, but pin the special ones up — warbirds (P-51, Spitfire, B-25, Corsair) → rare, iconic heavy bombers (B-17, B-29, Lancaster) → epic; vintage icons (DC-3, Ju-52, An-2, Beech 18, Constellation) → rare. A handful of named designators, exactly like the existing override list.

Caveat: ADS-B coverage of GA — especially warbirds and vintage — is patchy (many have no ADS-B Out, or broadcast on UAT). You’ll rarely actually catch one; but when you do it should be a real moment, and rarity tiers won’t flood the board.

Lean: add the warbird/vintage layer — it’s the GA mirror of the widebody refine you just approved.
