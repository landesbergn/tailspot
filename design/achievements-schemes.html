Tailspot — Achievements, four ways

Mockups for the Trophies-screen redesign. Styled to the app's real tokens (Brand.swift colors, the hex badge, the card language). The goal: a scheme that's game-friendly and stops the achievement badges from being conflated with the aircraft rarity + type pills.

The problem — three colored-badge systems collide

The app already spends two color systems on every plane. The trophy metals are a third — and trophy gold reads almost identical to legendary rarity gold. "Medals/Badges" as words also collide with these "badges."

1 · Rarity pills
COMMON UNCOMMON RARE EPIC ★ LEGENDARY
2 · Type pills
NNARROW WWIDE BBIZ
3 · Trophy metals (today)
BRONZE SILVER GOLD PLATINUM

Research takeaway (Khan Academy, Apple Fitness rosettes, Strava): disambiguate by shape + naming, not color — it survives grayscale & colorblindness. And for a collection/catching game, count-gated progression (Pokémon GO medals) is the proven fit.

My recommendation

Scheme 1 (Unified Levels). It's the only one that fixes all three problems at once: the cyan ring + level numeral kills the metal-vs-rarity-gold collision (no metals at all), "Achievements" as one pool kills the medals/badges word overload, and a single SPOTTER LVL + 15/24 header reads as "how far am I" instead of two disconnected counts. It keeps the count-gated climb that suits a catching game, needs no backend, and the secret trophies slot in as level-1 achievements. Scheme 2 is the cleanest long-term once the backend can serve real rarity %; Scheme 3 has the best precedent but is the riskiest on conflation.
